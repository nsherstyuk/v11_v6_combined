1. The Section 102 Rejection (Anticipation by Yagi)
The examiner is technically "right" from a high-level structural perspective. Yagi does indeed describe a multi-stage process:


Stage 1: Pulsed charging and discharging where charge = discharge (pure heating).


Stage 2: Pulsed charging and discharging where charge > discharge (heating + charging).


Stage 3: Normal continuous current charging.

Because your Claim 1 is written broadly, the examiner is mapping your "heating-optimized pulses" and "charging current" directly onto Yagi’s stages. To overcome this, you will likely need to narrow Claim 1 to include the specific technical differentiators that Yagi lacks—specifically the amplitude relationships found in your Claim 3.
